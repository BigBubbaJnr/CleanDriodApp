My final verdict

If CleanDroid launched today as a free app with lightweight ads, here's how I'd honestly position it:

Against CCleaner: I would recommend CleanDroid first. Not because it has every feature CCleaner has, but because I trust its direction more.
Against Files by Google: Files by Google will remain better integrated with Android for some tasks because it's built by Google, but CleanDroid has a stronger identity and can provide richer explanations.
Against SD Maid SE: SD Maid SE still has deeper filesystem capabilities, especially for advanced users using Shizuku or root. That's years of accumulated engineering. CleanDroid doesn't need to beat it at everything to succeed. Instead, it can own a different niche: the most transparent, approachable, and trustworthy storage analysis app.

I think the biggest opportunity now isn't another cleaning algorithm. It's making sure every person who installs CleanDroid immediately understands why it's different. If users come away thinking, "This app explains my storage instead of trying to scare me into paying," then you've built something with a genuinely distinctive place in the Android ecosystem.
